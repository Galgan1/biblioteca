# Knowledge Graph Report: Lote 1

## Overview
This report synthesizes the core philosophical, practical, and thematic concepts extracted from the first batch of skills (Lote 1). The analyzed corpus centers heavily on ancient philosophy, mythology, Greek religion, mystery cults, and the concept of philosophy as a way of life.

## Core Thematic Clusters

### 1. Philosophy as a Way of Life
*   **Key Sources:** Ambury ("Philosophy as a Way of Life"), Chaui ("Filosofia Modo de Vida"), Chase ("Did Socrates Meditate?").
*   **Concepts:** Practical philosophy, spiritual exercises, philosophical asceticism, Socrates' meditative practices. 

### 2. Greek Religion, Rituals, and Mystery Cults
*   **Key Sources:** Burkert ("Ancient Mystery Cults", "Greek Religion", "Babylon Memphis Persepolis"), Bowden ("Mystery Cults of the Ancient World"), Bremmer ("Initiation into the Mysteries"), Bernabé ("Redefining Dionysus", "Platão e o Orfismo", "Hieros Logos").
*   **Concepts:** Initiation rites, Orphism, Dionysian mysteries, esoteric religious practices, cross-cultural ancient religious exchange.

### 3. Mythology, Mythography, and the Hero's Journey
*   **Key Sources:** Campbell ("Masks of God", "O Poder do Mito", "Hero with a Thousand Faces", "Inner Reaches of Outer Space"), Buxton ("From Myth to Reason"), Brisson ("Filosofia do Mito"), Apolodoro ("Library of Greek Mythology").
*   **Concepts:** Monomyth, transition from mythos to logos, structure of Greek myths, psychological interpretations of mythological symbols.

### 4. Aristotelian Corpus
*   **Key Sources:** Aristóteles ("Problemata", "Elencos Sofísticos", "Constituição dos Atenienses", "História dos Animais VII-X", "Partes dos Animais").
*   **Concepts:** Natural philosophy, political history of Athens, biological taxonomy, logical fallacies.

### 5. Hellenistic and Ancient Texts & Magical Papyri
*   **Key Sources:** Betz ("Greek Magical Papyri"), Betegh ("Derveni Papyrus"), Barbieri ("Hinos Antigos"), Ateneu ("Deipnosophists").
*   **Concepts:** Ancient magic, symposiastic literature, cosmological interpretations, hymns.

### 6. Skepticism, Hellenistic Thought, and Eastern Connections
*   **Key Sources:** Brochard ("Céticos Gregos"), Beckwith ("Greek Buddha"), Cícero ("Nature of Gods / Academics").
*   **Concepts:** Pyrrhonism, academic skepticism, early connections between Greek philosophy and Buddhism.
